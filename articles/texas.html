The Ultimate Guide to Business
& Life in the Lone Star State

📅 2025 · 10 min read · Updated Monthly

Texas is more than a state — it's a way of life. The Lone Star State is the second largest state in America by both area and population, home to over 30 million people and some of the most diverse landscapes, cultures, and business opportunities in the entire country. From the Gulf Coast beaches to the West Texas desert, from the piney woods of East Texas to the rolling Hill Country, no two parts of Texas are alike.

Whether you're looking for trusted local businesses, planning a visit, or considering relocating your family or company — this guide covers everything you need to know about living and thriving in Texas.

🏙️ Texas's Major Cities

Texas is home to four of America's ten largest cities, each with its own distinct personality and business environment:

Houston — The Energy Capital of the World

With a population of over 2.3 million, Houston is the fourth largest city in America and the most diverse. Home to NASA's Johnson Space Center, the Port of Houston (the busiest port in the nation by total tonnage), and over 20 Fortune 500 headquarters, Houston is a true global city. The energy industry, healthcare (the Texas Medical Center is the world's largest medical complex), and aerospace drive the economy.

Dallas-Fort Worth — The DFW Metroplex

The DFW Metroplex is home to over 7.7 million people and is one of the fastest-growing regions in America. More Fortune 500 companies are headquartered here than any metro area outside New York City. The finance, technology, and transportation sectors thrive here, and the area added over 100,000 new residents in 2024 alone.

Austin — Silicon Hills

Austin has transformed into one of America's premier tech cities. Tesla, Apple, Oracle, and Amazon have all relocated major operations here. The University of Texas feeds a steady stream of talent into the workforce, and Austin's creative culture and live music scene make it one of the most livable cities in America.

San Antonio — Military City USA

San Antonio is the seventh largest city in America and home to five military installations — more than any other city in the nation. The tourism industry anchors the economy (the Alamo and River Walk draw millions annually), and healthcare and bioscience are rapidly growing sectors.

🌲 East Texas — The Piney Woods Region

East Texas is a special place. Defined by towering loblolly pine forests, winding rivers, quiet lakes, and communities where everyone knows your neighbor — East Texas has a charm that no other part of the state can match.

  • Nacogdoches — The oldest town in Texas, founded in 1779. Home to Stephen F. Austin State University, the Pineywoods Arts Festival, and a tight-knit business community that has served the region for generations.
  • Lufkin — Hub of Deep East Texas, known for its forestry and manufacturing heritage and a growing healthcare and retail sector.
  • Tyler — The Rose Capital of America. Tyler hosts the famous Texas Rose Festival every October and is the commercial center of East Texas with strong healthcare, retail, and education sectors.
  • Longview — A city rich in oil history that has diversified into manufacturing, retail, and healthcare. The Gregg County area serves as a regional hub for employment and commerce.

⭐ Top Attractions in Texas

  • The Alamo, San Antonio — the most visited historic site in Texas
  • Space Center Houston — experience the history of human spaceflight
  • Big Bend National Park — West Texas wilderness unlike anywhere on earth
  • Galveston Island — beaches, Pleasure Pier, and the historic Strand
  • South Padre Island — the best beach destination on the Texas Gulf Coast
  • The River Walk, San Antonio — 15 miles of shops, restaurants, and culture
  • Sixth Floor Museum, Dallas — the JFK assassination site and museum
  • Guadalupe Mountains National Park — the highest peak in Texas
  • Texas State Aquarium, Corpus Christi — Gulf of Mexico marine life
  • Enchanted Rock, Fredericksburg — a massive pink granite dome in the Hill Country

💼 Why Texas is the Best State for Business

  • No state income tax — both personal and corporate
  • Business-friendly regulations and permitting
  • Lower cost of living and doing business than coastal states
  • Massive and growing consumer market of 30+ million people
  • World-class infrastructure including highways, rail, and 27 public airports
  • Diverse economy — energy, technology, healthcare, agriculture, defense, and tourism
  • Strong small business community and culture of entrepreneurship
  • Year-round warm climate that supports outdoor industries

🍽️ Texas Food Culture

Texas food is a religion. BBQ reigns supreme — from brisket smoked low and slow in Central Texas to the beef ribs of the Hill Country. Tex-Mex is its own culinary tradition, distinct from Mexican food and deeply woven into the Texas identity. Chicken fried steak, kolaches (brought by Czech immigrants), Gulf Coast seafood, and Blue Bell ice cream are all part of what makes eating in Texas a unique experience.

🌟 Texas Fun Facts

  • Texas was an independent republic from 1836 to 1846 before joining the United States
  • The state is so large that El Paso is closer to Los Angeles than it is to Houston
  • Texas produces more wind energy than any other state in America
  • The state has the most farms of any state — over 248,000
  • Texas shares 1,254 miles of border with Mexico
  • "Remember the Alamo" is the most famous battle cry in Texas history
